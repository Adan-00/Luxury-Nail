Aftercare

How to care for your nails

Following these simple tips will help your nails last longer and stay looking fresh.

‚ú¶

Don‚Äôt use nails as tools

Avoid opening cans, scratching surfaces, or picking at hard objects with your nails.

Why it matters: using nails as tools causes stress on the apex and leads to lifting.

  • Use keys/spoons instead of nails
  • Avoid peeling stickers or picking
  • Be extra careful in the first 24h after your set
‚ú¶

Wear gloves for cleaning

Harsh chemicals and hot water can weaken your nails and cause lifting.

Best practice: gloves protect from hot water + detergents.

  • Washing-up liquid breaks down product over time
  • Hot water expands/loosens product edges
  • Gloves = longer retention
‚ú¶

Use cuticle oil daily

Applying cuticle oil keeps your nails flexible, healthy, and glossy.

How to use: one drop per hand, massage around cuticle area.

  • Best time: before bed
  • Helps prevent dryness + lifting
  • Makes the set look fresher for longer
‚ú¶

Book infills on time

We recommend infills every 2‚Äì3 weeks to maintain structure and prevent breakage.

Why: as your nail grows, the balance shifts and break risk increases.

  • Infills keep strength in the right place
  • Prevents lifting turning into full breaks
  • Keeps nails looking clean + fresh

Reminder: Proper aftercare = longer-lasting nails and better results.
